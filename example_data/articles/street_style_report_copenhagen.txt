Street Style Report: Copenhagen Fashion Week AW24

Copenhagen Fashion Week has cemented itself as the street style capital of the European circuit. Where Milan and Paris skew toward polished editorial fantasy, Copenhagen's pavement serves as a live laboratory for how progressive consumers actually dress — and crucially, how they want to be seen.

The dominant silhouette this season was a deliberate collision of structure and ease. Oversized tailored blazers in grey flannel and camel were worn over micro shorts or with nothing beneath — a proportion play that surfaced repeatedly among the Scandinavian attendees. The contrast communicates mastery rather than confusion, suggesting a wearer confident enough to break the rules of suiting without abandoning them entirely.

Footwear told its own story. The chunky trainer continues its long run, but the silhouette is shifting: the bubble toe is deflating in favour of a longer, more elongated last. Clogs — both wooden-soled heritage styles and platform reinterpretations in leather and suede — appeared consistently, particularly among the Danish and Swedish contingent. Read as a continued domesticity hangover from the pandemic years, now aestheticised.

Bag culture outside the venue was notably practical. The tote — in canvas, leather, and technical nylon — dominated over the micro-bag that had its moment in 2021–2022. Functionality is the flex. Brands responding to this shift include Toteme, whose canvas totes were ubiquitous, and A.P.C., whose half-moon shoulder bag appeared in nearly every colour of its range.

Colour in the street was cooler than the runway would suggest: forest green, chocolate brown, and off-white formed the backbone. Where colour was used assertively, it was singular — a cobalt coat over an all-neutral outfit, rather than polychrome mixing.

The Copenhagen street style consumer is the most reliable leading indicator for the premium-to-mid market's next twelve months. Brands tracking social data from CPHFW week consistently observe a 6–8 month lag before these signals surface in mainstream retail.
