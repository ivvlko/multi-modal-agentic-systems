Spring/Summer 2024 Colour Trends

The Spring/Summer 2024 season is defined by a palette that oscillates between earthy warmth and electric optimism. After several seasons dominated by muted neutrals, colour is returning with intention — each hue carrying a distinct mood and cultural signal.

Peach Fuzz, Pantone's Colour of the Year, anchors the season. It reads simultaneously warm and gentle, pairing effortlessly with cream, warm white, and terracotta. Designers from Jacquemus to Loewe have incorporated the tone across knitwear, tailoring, and accessories, signalling a collective pivot toward softness after years of maximalist provocation.

Cobalt blue is the season's power statement. Seen prominently at Valentino and Bottega Veneta, it functions as a modern-day primary — assertive without aggression. Its versatility across silk, denim, and leather makes it one of the most commercially viable trend colours of the cycle.

Matcha green continues its trajectory from emerging accent to mainstream wardrobe staple. Rooted in the broader wellness aesthetic that has permeated fashion since 2021, the tone resonates strongly with the 25–40 demographic who seek clothing that signals calm authority. Expect it to perform well in tailored separates and outerwear.

Butter yellow rounds out the optimistic quadrant of the palette. Lighter and more wearable than the acid yellows of 2022, it works as a natural bridge between the neutrals and the brights — accessible to a broader consumer base and particularly strong in womenswear.

Trend intelligence note: brands should monitor the cobalt-to-peach ratio in social commerce data. Early indicators from TikTok and Instagram Reels suggest cobalt outperforms among the 18–24 segment, while peach indexes higher for 30–45. Stocking decisions should reflect this bifurcation.
